Breaking Intuition

When I walked into my first day of physics class in high school, I carried with me a set of ideas which I learned from simply observing and interacting with the world. In fact everyone builds up what they believe to be intuitive concepts, whether it be in science, math, or any other field. Without any scientific training whatsoever, we begin to build intuition. If you let go of a ball in the air, what will happen? If you try to run on the ice of a frozen lake, will it be easier than running on the sidewalk? If you stand in the sun and on the ground you see a strange dark misshapen copy of yourself imitating your every move… who is following you?
