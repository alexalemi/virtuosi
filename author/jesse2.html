Many people consider the air escaping from a tire as a classic example of an adiabatic process. What is an adiabatic process? It is a process that happens so quickly there is no time for heat flow to occur. For our air in the bike tire this means we’re letting it out of the tire so quickly that no energy can move into it from the surrounding air.
