Warning: picture has little or no relation to this post.

I realized the other day that I’ve seen a lot of people talk about research results, but it is much more rare that I see someone talk about how we do research. I think that may be because, to us as scientists, the process is second nature. We’ve been doing it for years. Other folks may be less familiar with the process though. With this in mind, I’m going to do a short series of posts focused on how we do an experiment. Not the results, not so much the physics, but the process that we go through to create, setup, and carry out an experiment.
