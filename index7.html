Stated more formally the problem is this: given a needle of length l and a floor of parallel lines separated by a distance d, what is the probability of a randomly dropped needle crossing a line? Working this all out (see a derivation here, for example), we find that the probability a needle crosses a line is $$ P = \frac{2l}{d\pi} $$ So now we have a nice way of experimentally coming up with a value for pi.
