I wanted to come up with something interesting to do with it, but first I had to see how it did on some basic tests. It turns out that the tests gave really interesting results themselves! A drop test gave clean results and a spring test gave fantastic data; however a pendulum test gave some problems. You might guess the accelerometer would give a reading of 0 in all axes when the device is sitting on a desk. However, this accelerometer measures “proper acceleration,” which essentially is a measure of acceleration relative to free-fall. So the device will read -1 in the z direction (in units where 1 corresponds to 9.8 m/s^2, the acceleration due to gravity at the surface of Earth).
